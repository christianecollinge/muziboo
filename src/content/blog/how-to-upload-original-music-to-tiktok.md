## The Official Route: How to Upload Original Music to TikTok Via a Distributor

For musicians looking to officially release their tracks and have them available in TikTok's commercial music library, the primary method involves using a digital music distributor. These services act as the bridge between your finished track and the vast ecosystem of streaming platforms, including TikTok. Think of them as your record label without the fuss, handling the intricate backend work of licensing, metadata, and royalty collection. This is the most professional way to ensure your music is properly licenced for commercial use on the platform, allowing other creators to use your sound in their videos and you to potentially earn royalties.

When you sign up with a distributor like DistroKid, CD Baby, TuneCore, or many others, you'll upload your final mixed and mastered track (or tracks). During the distribution process, you'll select which platforms you want your music to be sent to, and crucially, TikTok will be one of the options. Once approved and processed, which can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks, your song will appear in TikTok's commercial sound library. This means that any user on TikTok can then search for your track and incorporate it into their videos, boosting its reach and visibility. This route is ideal for finished pieces you're ready to share with the world, establishing your presence professionally across all major digital outlets.

### Choosing the Right Distributor for Your UK Sound

With a plethora of digital distributors available, selecting the right one can feel a bit daunting. For UK artists, it’s worth considering a few factors beyond just the cost. Look at the range of services offered: do they provide clear royalty reports? Is their customer support responsive? Do they have good analytical tools that can help you understand where your music is being heard? Some distributors offer unlimited releases for an annual fee, which can be fantastic value for prolific bedroom producers. Others might take a percentage cut of your royalties but offer more extensive marketing tools or artist support.

While most major distributors serve the global market efficiently, ensuring your music reaches TikTok users in the UK and beyond, it’s always wise to read reviews and perhaps chat with other independent artists in our Muziboo community to see which services they've had success with. Ultimately, the best distributor is one that aligns with your specific needs, budget, and long-term artistic goals, allowing you to focus more on the craft of making music and less on the logistics of distribution.

## The DIY Approach: How to Upload Original Music to TikTok Directly (Using Your Own Content)

Beyond the official distribution channels, there’s a more immediate, direct, and incredibly popular method for getting your original music onto TikTok: simply using it as the background audio for your own videos. This is the DIY spirit in action, perfectly aligning with the ethos of Muziboo. You don't need a distributor for this; you just need your music file and the TikTok app. When you create a video on TikTok, you have the option to record audio directly, or even better, to upload a video that already has your music baked into its soundtrack.

To do this, you'll create a video using your phone or editing software, incorporating a snippet of your original track as the accompanying audio. When you upload this video to TikTok, that audio becomes "original sound" linked to your profile. Other users can then click on your video's sound, see it originated from you, and even choose to use that same sound in their own videos. This method is brilliant for testing out new ideas, sharing short instrumental hooks, or giving a glimpse into your creative process. It’s perfect for those raw, unpolished fragments you’re experimenting with and serves as a fantastic way to gauge initial reactions from a broad audience.
